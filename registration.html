Registration

Registering the documents related to the transfer, sale or lease of a property is mandatory by law under Section 17 of the Indian Registration Act, 1908. Particularly for the state of Maharashtra, the registration of an agreement to buy or sell an apartment is compulsory under Section 4 of the Maharashtra Ownership Flats Act, 1963. A person in whose favour the property is registered becomes its lawful owner.

You Should Know

An occupancy certificate is a document issued by a local municipal or building department authority, which certifies the compliance of a building with relevant building codes and its suitability for occupancy. This certificate is issued once the authority ensures that the building follows certain safety norms and has all the basic amenities, and is subject to change, based on the laws of the State.

A Completion Certificate is an essential document attesting that a building has been constructed as per the safety norms and guidelines of the Buildings Act. This certificate contains all the information about the project – from the building height to the quality of the construction material used.

Completion Certificate establishes that the building construction has been carried out in compliance with all building rules and standards such as the Floor Area Ratio (FAR), structural design and layout, construction quality and distance from the road and surrounding buildings, among others.
